Shah Mohammad Fahim is a physician turned nutrition scientist with training in epidemiology and data science. He is the author of the book কল্প-ডাক্তার..

Fahim is an Associate Scientist at the International Centre for Diarrhoeal Disease Research, Bangladesh (icddr,b), and is pursuing his PhD at Cornell University, USA.

He has published articles in The American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, The Journal of Infectious Diseases, The American Journal of Tropical Medicine and Hygiene, PLoS Neglected Tropical Diseases, Public Health Nutrition, and The New England Journal of Medicine.

He lives in Ithaca, New York with his wife.

About Me

I am a physician with training and experience in nutrition, epidemiology, and infectious diseases. I am a doctoral trainee in the Division of Nutritional Sciences at Cornell University. Before coming to Cornell, I worked in the Nutrition and Clinical Services Division at the International Centre for Diarrhoeal Disease Research, Bangladesh (icddr,b). During my stint as icddr,b, I led and supported several research projects on nutrition, enteric infections, and intestinal inflammation in different population subsets including infants, children, and adults. Currently, I am involved in randomized clinical trials focusing on nutritional interventions in children and women of reproductive age living in high-burden settings. Our team is working to leverage data from these trials to develop non-invasive, cheap, and easy-to-administer biomarkers for the assessment of nutritional impairments and inflammatory changes in diverse populations. We are also working to investigate the effect of diet-based approaches on the biomarker and gut microbiome profiles in children and reproductive-age women living in resource-poor settings.

Scientific Contributions

1. Nutrition, immune function, infection, and inflammation in the context of Maternal and Child Health: My work in this area is focused on the biological interplay between nutrition, infection, and inflammation, particularly in children and women of reproductive age. My recent works are focused on elucidating the pathogenesis of environmental enteric dysfunction (EED) and determining the consequences of this sub-clinical disorder during the early years of life. I am also involved in projects with the goal of assessing non-invasive biomarkers of EED in low-income settings.
